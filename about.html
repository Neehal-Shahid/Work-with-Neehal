How I Think

Not a bio. A working philosophy.

I chose WordPress because ownership matters. Roughly 43% of the web runs on it — not because it's trendy, but because you can take your site anywhere, forever. You're not locked into my tools, my hosting, or my goodwill. You log in, you edit, you leave if you want. That portability is the point.

I approach every project from the outside in. Before I ask about colors or plugins, I ask who your customer is and what they need to believe in the first five seconds. Most developers design inward — from the CMS outward. I design from that first impression back to the homepage.

What you get is not a beautiful website for its own sake. You get a business asset — something with a job to do and a way to tell if it's doing it. If it doesn't help phones ring, forms fill, or trust stick, it isn't finished.

What I Value

The non-negotiables.

Clarity

I don't have jargon conversations with clients. If I can't explain what I'm doing in plain English, I probably don't understand it well enough.

Ownership

Your website should belong to you — not to me, not to my tools. I use WordPress because you can take it anywhere, forever.

Reliability

If I say Tuesday, it's Tuesday. Not "sometime this week." Deadlines aren't suggestions.

Craft

I care about the gap between good and very good. That 10% that most clients never see but their customers always feel.
