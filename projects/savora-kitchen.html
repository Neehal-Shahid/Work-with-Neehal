Restaurant

Savora Kitchen

A restaurant site with visual menu, booking CTA, and mobile-first speed.

The Challenge

What the business needed.

The owner had a Facebook page and a PDF menu. No website. Meanwhile, competitors were taking reservations online while Savora was still asking people to call during dinner rush.

They needed something that looked as good as the food — and worked on a phone, where most restaurant searches actually happen.

The Approach

How it was solved.

We built a full WordPress site around the reservation. Visual menu, photo gallery, Google/Yelp widgets, and a Reserve a Table CTA that shows up on every page without feeling pushy.

Mobile came first. Typography, tap targets, and image weights were tuned so the site feels fast on cellular — not just on Wi-Fi in the office.

Local SEO basics went in at launch: NAP consistency, schema for the restaurant, and pages search engines can actually understand.
